## Standards applied to every post

- Primary keyword in the title, H1, meta description and first paragraph.
- The first paragraph answers the question directly and completely (this is what AI Overviews and answer engines lift and cite).
- Clear H2 and H3 structure phrased around the questions buyers ask.
- An FAQ block with FAQPage schema, matched honestly to visible questions.
- BlogPosting schema, named author, published and updated dates (freshness helps AI citation; roughly half of content cited in AI answers is under thirteen weeks old, so refresh cornerstones on a schedule).
- Internal links to the relevant money pages (models, sectors, services, configurator, quote).
- A clean, self-contained meta description (no truncation, no em-dashes).
- Real figures only. Where a number is useful and not on the site, source it (for example the Energy Saving Trust for running costs) or keep the point qualitative. Never invent stats, clients or testimonials.

## Suggested order and cadence

1. **In parallel from the start:** run the Part A audit on the 10 live posts, resolving the resorts and hotels duplication first and verifying the road-legal post for accuracy.
2. **First net-new posts:** B1 (branding), B2 (servicing and warranty), B3 (insurance). These are the differentiators plus the strongest untapped term.
3. **Second wave:** B4 to B9 (sector gaps, battery care, bespoke buying guide, model chooser).
4. **Ongoing:** B10 destination guides for the top markets, and B11 from the question pool once pulled.
5. **Refresh:** revisit the cornerstone posts (cost, road-legal, lithium vs lead-acid, running cost) on a regular cycle and update the dates, since freshness supports both rankings and AI citation.

A realistic cadence is two to four net-new posts a month while the audit pass runs, rather than a burst. Consistency and depth matter more than volume.
