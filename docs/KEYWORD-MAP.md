# KEYWORD MAP — Electric Buggies

**Version 2.0.** Reconciles the SEMrush keyword research with the live site architecture (range, sectors, services, locations, guides). Every cluster maps to a real page. Where I hold SEMrush data the volume and difficulty (KD) are shown; where I do not, the row is marked **[research]** with my best judgement on intent and priority, plus the exact pull needed to confirm it.

Pairs with the scored keyword tracker (xlsx) and the SEO Strategy Playbook.

---

## How to read this

- **Volume** is average UK searches per month. **KD** is difficulty (0 to 100, lower is easier).
- **Intent** reflects the real search result page, not just the tool label. Note: SEMrush tags many buggy terms "informational", but their result pages are commercial, so we treat them as commercial or transactional.
- **Priority:** P1 build and optimise first, P2 strong second wave, P3 nice to have, Skip do not target.
- **[research]** means pull this in SEMrush to get volume and KD before committing effort.

---

## Three decisions that affect this whole map

1. **Premium voice vs search volume.** The brand leads with "electric vehicles" and "no golf carts here". Correct for positioning, but the volume is in "electric golf buggy", "golf buggies for sale", "golf cart". Resolution applied throughout: keep the premium voice in H1s and hero copy, and place the high volume search terms in title tags, meta descriptions, H2s, body copy, image alt text and FAQ. This captures the traffic without diluting the brand. The money pages (range, model, sector) are the priority for this fix because they currently favour brand voice over search terms.

2. **Road-legal is a compliance-led cluster, not a category claim.** Standard buggies are private-land only and are not road-legal in standard form. Only type-approved or specific single-seat models qualify. So road-legal keywords map to the regulations guide and to genuinely approved models, never to a blanket "road-legal buggies" category. High intent and very low KD, so worth owning carefully and accurately.

3. **No single-seater model exists** (range starts at The Two). The single-seater cluster (KD 0 to 3) and the road-legal single-seat angle have nowhere to land. Options: add a single-seat or road-legal model, route this demand through Bespoke and the regulations guide, or skip the cluster. Product decision needed.

## Cluster 9 — Local and international

**Pages:** `/locations/[place]` (Dubai, Scotland, Bermuda, New York, USA, Abu Dhabi, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Switzerland, Monaco, French Riviera, Marbella, Lake Como, Algarve, Maldives, Mauritius, Singapore, Australia, Bahamas).

Two logics operate here, and they are different:

- **Premium-destination targeting (primary).** The location pages target high-value estate, resort and luxury markets, not high search volume. Success is capturing wealthy buyers in those markets, supported by unique localised content and Service plus areaServed schema. Keep each page genuinely localised (climate, terrain, estate and resort context, delivery and lead time); thin or duplicated pages will hurt.
- **Search-term geography (secondary).** For organic volume, the UK, Ireland and Australia share the term "buggy" (Australia matches UK volume for "golf buggy"); the US and much of the world search "golf cart". If you want US organic traffic, the US pages and any US-facing content need "golf cart" terms, not "buggy". Pull `golf cart` and `electric golf cart` to size this.

Map per page: [model or sector] plus [place], for example "electric buggies Dubai", "resort buggies Algarve", "estate utility vehicle Scotland". Most are low volume; treat as conversion and credibility plays with correct schema, and confirm the localised copy is unique.
