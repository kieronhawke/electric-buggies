## 1. The business (context)
- **What it is:** a premium UK dealer of **electric buggies / utility & passenger vehicles** (golf-buggy-style EVs). Customers browse → configure/brand → request a tailored quote → bespoke build → sourced from a Chinese manufacturer (Suzhou Eagle) → delivered in the UK and internationally.
- **Goal:** become the UK's **#1 premium electric-buggy dealer**, expanding beyond golf into estates, resorts/hotels, events/festivals, airports, holiday parks, film/TV, and international luxury markets.
- **Differentiators (lead with these in content):** best-in-class premium brand/website (Land Rover/Porsche/Tesla feel); **bespoke configuration + custom branding** (logo on bodywork, fleet liveries); **extended warranty**; a **24-hour VIP technical call-out** team (UK + worldwide, experts come to you within 24–48h); cost-effective vs competitors ("we aim to beat any genuine like-for-like quote"); British marque, built to order.
- **Commercial model:** every CTA is **"request a tailored quote"** (bespoke + B2B) — **never** e-commerce checkout. Also **hire** (events/fleets) and **service plans**.
- **Brand name:** "Electric Buggies" (provisional placeholder wordmark; could change — keep content brand-name-light where easy, or easily updatable).
- **Live site:** https://electric-buggies.vercel.app (custom domain pending — see §10). CMS is **Sanity** (all content editable; hosted Studio + embedded /studio).

## 2. Positioning & voice (apply to ALL content)
- **Tone:** confident British premium marque. Warm but not over-eager. Benefit-led, concrete, human. Think Land Rover/Porsche/Tesla editorial.
- **Hard rules:**
  - **No em-dashes (—) anywhere.** Use full stops, commas, "and", or brackets. (There is a build guard; keep copy clean.)
  - **No "AI tells"** — vary sentence length, cut throat-clearing ("In today's world…", "Whether you're…"), avoid repetitive triads and over-balanced "not X but Y", no clichés.
  - **No fabricated content** — no invented testimonials, client names, stats, or case studies. (Brand has no real clients yet; testimonials must be genuine or clearly illustrative.)
  - **British English** throughout.
  - Weave keywords **naturally** — headings, intros, alt text, meta — never stuff.
- **Compliance care in copy (accuracy matters for trust + ASA):**
  - Standard buggies are **for private land** (estates, resorts, golf courses, parks). They are **not** road-legal in standard form.
  - **Road use** requires reclassification/type approval (category L / quadricycle-type), with brakes, lights, indicators, windscreen etc.; some **single-seat** models are road-legal. Airports, resorts, National Trust etc. have **operational-area** allowances.
  - So: market private-land use confidently; only claim "road legal" for genuinely approved models; mention IVA/DVLA/type-approval where relevant. Don't overstate.
  - "Beat any quote" phrasing must be truthful and hedged ("we aim to beat any genuine like-for-like quote").
